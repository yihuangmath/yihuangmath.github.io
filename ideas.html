Crack-pot ideas

Here're some totally crack-pot ideas that I'd like to try:

Explicitly constructing holomorphic differentials

In this short note, I describe a way of constructing a Riemann surface \(S\) with a holomorphic differential \(\omega\) defined on \(S\), so that \(\omega\) is naturally described in terms of the Weierstrass \(\wp\) function. I have the niggling suspicion that all such pairs \((S,\omega)\) may be constructed using similar Weierstrass \(\wp\)-functions based constructions, but my complex analysis isn't quite up to scratch for me to know how to tackle this. I suspect that I'll need to prove a uniformization theorem of sorts for this to be plausible.

A strange type of group cohomology

In this pdf, I describe a weird type of group homology that philosophically regards an arbitrary abstract group \(G\) as a Lie group and then tries to do some sort of deRham cohomology on \(G\). I haven't touched this in a while, but I think that some classical group theoretic invariants are encoded as cohomologies in this way. I really should take another look at this...

Less crack-pot ideas

Uniformisation of bordered hyperbolic surfaces

Uniformisation is magical, and I'm reasonably convinced that there should be a way to uniformize a smoothly bordered Riemann surface \(S\) to produce an interval's worth of (normalised) constant curvature surface metrics \(\{g_t\}_{t\in[0,1]}\) compatible with the complex structure of \(S\), so that \(g_0\) is a hyperbolic surface with \(0\) boundary geodesic curvature and \(g_1\) is a flat metric with constant (negative) constant boundary geodesic curvature. If anyone feels that they're decent with elliptic partial differential equations with (fairly simple) nonlinear boundary conditions, PLEASE CONTACT ME.

Teichmüller theory of hyperbolic surfaces with right-angled borders

In Chapter 2 of my PhD thesis, I describe the Teichmüller space and the moduli space of hyperbolic surfaces with ideal points on their (geodesic) borders. It turns out that these guys are still naturally symplectic via the Weil-Petersson form that I define, although they do wind up having infinite volume. I'm reasonably confident that something like this should hold also for Teichmüller spaces and moduli spaces of hyperbolic surfaces with broken geodesic borders which are broken at right-angles. Although, there's some need to constrain the boundary lengths somehow to retain the symplecticness.

A new expression for the Weil-Petersson form

Also in my thesis, I describe generalised \(\lambda\)-length coordinates for the (hypercycle-decorated) Teichmüller space of (close geodesic) bordered hyperbolic surfaces. Using these coordinates, I can, for the once-punctured torus \(S_{1,1}\), construct a mapping class group invariant 2-form on the decorated Teichmüller space \(\hat{\mathcal{T}}(S_{1,1},L)\). Unfortunately, it's not the Weil-Petersson form. So, I really really really want to bash out the actual Weil-Petersson form and compare the two.

Generalising Markoff's (quadratic forms) theorem

In this, I skim through the basics for how the geometry of a particular 1-cusped torus may be used to derive a characterisation theorem for quadratic forms (by Markoff). At the end of this short note, I give a slight generalisation of this theorem obtained by conjugating the specific monodromy representation used in the proof of the result. I suggest two potential ideas for further generalising this result - the first involves using a different 1-cusped tori, and the second method is based on possibly using the geometry of 3-cusped projective planes (which are surprisingly similar to 1-cusped tori, as highlighted in Chapter 4 of my thesis) to obtain analogous results.
