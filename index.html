Who am I?

My name is Yi Huang. Last year (17th of December 2014), I received my PhD from the University of Melbourne under the supervision of A/Prof. Paul Norbury. I am a geometer/geometric topologist, and much of my work involves using the geometry of hyperbolic surfaces to study Teichmüller and moduli space theory. Here's my thesis, and here's my CV. I'll be starting a postdoc position at the Yau Mathematical Sciences Center at Tsinghua University (it's in Beijing) at the end of the year.

Why did I make this website?

I have fairly broad mathematical interests and I'd love to hear about your work. Who knows, maybe we can collaborate on something!

What have I been up to?

I recently visited a bunch of people in Singapore and Osaka thanks to the Australian Mathematics Society's Lift-Off Travelling Fellowship. I visited Prof. Ser Peow Tan and Dr. Hengnan Hu at the National University of Singapore and A/Prof. Hideki Miyachi at Osaka University. I had a pretty good time learning maths from them, and gave a few seminars about Paul and my work on Markoff quads as well as some of my thesis work on crowned hyperbolic surfaces. I'm currently busying myself tutoring at the University of Melbourne, whilst trying to pump out some preprints.

What are my plans?

I've been quite fortunate to receive two travelling awards: the 2014/2015 AustMS Lift-Off Travelling Fellowships and the 2015 AK Head Travelling Scholarship for Mathematical Scientists. I've already used up my Lift-Off Fellowship, and I intend to use my AK Head Mathematical Sciences to visit A/Prof. LaFountain at Western Illinois University as well as Prof. Athanase Papadopoulos and Prof. Greg McShane in France mid-2015.

What am I reading?
